2. Colonialismo de Asentamiento: Un Sistema de Genocidio, Violencia y Despojo

1. Definición: Una Estructura de Eliminación y Reemplazo.

El colonialismo de asentamiento es descrito no como un evento histórico aislado, sino como un sistema y una estructura continua. Su lógica fundamental es el desplazamiento y reemplazo de la población nativa por una nueva sociedad de colonos, lo que lo diferencia de otras formas de colonialismo que se centran principalmente en la extracción de recursos.

  • Settler colonialism is a system of logic and structural displacement. The us is guilty of this as well with the mass killings of native americans.
  • “settler-colonialism seeks to replace a native population with western-aligned immigrants through extreme ethnic-based violence. Think the united states, canada, australia and south africa. They all committed appalling crimes against their indigenous populations.”.
  • Settler colonialism is a structure not an event. You benefit off of indigenous loss and oppression. These things won’t disappear with positive thinking.
  • Settler colonialism is a particular type of colonialism involving sending in settlers to cultivate/control territory instead of the most common pattern of dominating people.
  • No matter where it occurs in the world, settler colonialism always has two aims: take the land and disappear the natives.

2. La Conexión Inherente con el Genocidio.

Un tema recurrente es la afirmación de que el colonialismo de asentamiento es, por su propia naturaleza, genocida. Se argumenta que el objetivo de reemplazar a una población requiere inevitablemente su eliminación, ya sea a través de la violencia directa, la enfermedad, la inanición o el borrado cultural sistemático.

  • Settler colonialism is genocide. Invading a place, killing the indigenous ppl there (through murder, disease or starvation) and stealing their homes and lands is not good.
  • Hey look it's the settler part of the settler colonialism. Which as always is built on genocide.
  • Settler-colonialism is based on genocides. Always has been. And note the plural; it's important to remember that it's not just one. It's hundreds and hundreds of genocides, for centuries and centuries, up to and including today.
  • It’s my belief that settler colonialism is inherently genocidal. We have been funding that since 1949.
  • Un special rapporteur francesca albanese: "settler colonialism is intimately, quintessentially genocidal, because it will result, sooner or later, in the annihilation of the indigenous people if it succeeds. Look at the united states.".

3. Un Proceso Continuo, No un Evento del Pasado.

Las publicaciones enfatizan que el colonialismo de asentamiento no debe ser visto como un capítulo cerrado de la historia. Se describe como un sistema activo y continuo cuyas estructuras y consecuencias perduran en el presente, afectando la vida diaria, las políticas gubernamentales y las relaciones sociales en las naciones fundadas sobre este modelo.

  • Colonialism isn’t history — it’s alive in daily life. Settlers and workers don’t realize they’re part of the ongoing erasure, the silent genocide, of indigenous peoples.
  • Settler colonialism is in itself, by definition, ongoing, active genocide. As long as the imperatives of colonial society reign, nothing is past. The genocides continue.
  • Pretending settler colonialism isn't an issue "because time has passed", as if it isn't the foundation of the very capitalism, binary gender, racism ect they fake like they are fighting against.
  • The way settler colonialism is commonly misunderstood as a long-ago event rather than a system robs it of almost all of its explanatory potential.
  • Settler colonialism is not an event, is a ongoing process and we are still living it!

4. Consecuencias: Despojo, Racismo y Borrado Cultural.

Más allá de la violencia física, se detallan las múltiples capas de opresión que conlleva el colonialismo de asentamiento. Estas incluyen el robo de tierras y recursos, la imposición de jerarquías raciales y la destrucción deliberada de la identidad de los pueblos colonizados, abarcando su historia, lengua, espiritualidad y sentido de autoestima.

  • Settler colonialism always returns to its roots: racism, dispossession, marginalization, genocide.
