26. Proyectos de Ley Anti-Semitismo: Entre la Lucha Contra el Odio y la Censura

1. Definiciones y Controversias en la Legislación Anti-Semitismo.

Las propuestas legislativas para combatir el antisemitismo han generado un intenso debate sobre la definición del término y su posible impacto en la libertad de expresión, especialmente en relación con las críticas al gobierno de Israel.

  • "the bill would require the department of education to use the controversial international holocaust remembrance alliance definition of antisemitism — which classifies most anti-zionism as antisemitic — when investigating allegations of discrimination.".
  • "this bill isn't about fighting antisemitism; it’s about weaponizing it to silence dissent. It would codify a discredited definition of antisemitism that deliberately conflates criticism of israel with antisemitism, criminalizing advocacy for palestinian rights and chilling free speech on campuses.".
  • "this bill is more likely to stoke antisemitism than to quell it. But it's also breathtakingly stupid.".

2. El Debate sobre la IHRA y la Libertad de Expresión.

La definición de antisemitismo de la Alianza Internacional para el Recuerdo del Holocausto (IHRA) es un punto central de controversia, ya que algunos argumentan que equipara la crítica a Israel con el antisemitismo, lo que podría restringir la libertad de expresión.

  • "the bill mentions that they want to start using the ihra definition of anti semitism, which includes quite a few exemple of critics against israel being considered antisemitism. This is obviously bullshit to protect a genocidal and fascist regime.".
  • "s558 —"antisemitism awareness act"—will codify a definition of antisemitism into law that conflates criticism of israeli state actions & ideologies w/ anti-jewish bigotry.".
  • "confront the n.j. State assembly on dangerous antisemitism bill the bill would accept the international holocaust remembrance alliance’s working definition of antisemitism, which conflates criticism of the state of israel and zionism with antisemitism.".

3. Acusaciones de Censura y Ataques a la Libertad Académica.

Las leyes propuestas han sido criticadas por grupos de derechos civiles y uniones de profesores, quienes argumentan que podrían utilizarse para censurar discusiones sobre el conflicto israelí-palestino y reprimir la libertad académica.

  • "ca folks, ab 715 is a bill which if passed will censor the teaching of true history and current events. It will lead to costly litigation that taxpayers will foot the bill for. The bill equates calling israel a settler colonial state with antisemitism. If you oppose this bill, let your reps know.".
  • "“repackaging censorship under the guise of combating antisemitism does a disservice to the very real fight against hate. We already have laws protecting students from discrimination. Ab 715 would effectively silence educators and erase palestinian voices.”".
  • "policing teachers! ca lawmakers back censorship disguised as antisemitism prevention despite educator opposition, ca senate/assembly passed ab 715 at night to establish "antisemitism" prevention coordinator to police teachers during israel’s genocides.".

4. Excepciones y Controversias Adicionales.

La inclusión de cláusulas de "libertad religiosa" que protegen declaraciones antisemitas, como la acusación de que "los judíos mataron a Jesús", ha generado indignación y críticas, cuestionando la sinceridad de los esfuerzos para combatir el antisemitismo.

  • "the antisemitism awareness act is supposed to make criticizing israel illegal. However, senate republicans ironically added a religious liberty clause to protect christians who say “the jews killed christ. That’s literally the og antisemitic trope.".
  • "pretty wild that a bill allegedly targeting antisemitism provides a carve out to perpetuate an antisemitic conspiracy theory. Almost like this bill isn’t about protecting jews.".
  • "how can a bill fight antisemitism while protecting the right to call jews “christ killers”? prof. Ian lustick (university of pennsylvania) explains how the antisemitism awareness act wouldn’t stop real antisemitism, but would instead criminalize criticism of israel.".
