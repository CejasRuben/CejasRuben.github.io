28. Homofobia: Del Meme Viral a la Realidad en el Arte Digital

1. El Meme Viral: Confundiendo Heterocromia con Homofobia.

Una de las tendencias más destacadas es un meme originado por la confusión del término 'heterocromia' (ojos de diferente color) con 'homofobia'. La frase "beautiful cat with homophobia" se viralizó, llevando a la comunidad a referirse humorísticamente a personajes con esta característica como si tuvieran "homofobia en los ojos".

  • Omfg. It's heterochromia. Heterochromia, not homophobia. You can't be serious.
  • Lol it's a reference to this post where someone said "homophobia" when they meant "heterochromia".
  • I am so used to the further bastardization of “homophobia in his eyes” that i forgot the actual meme is heterophobia.
  • Will "homophobia in its eyes" instead of using the term heterochromia ever stop making me giggle.
  • "homophobia" is fear of gays and lesbians. The word you want is "heterochromia", literally "mixed colors".

2. La 'Homofobia' como Obstáculo Irónico y Creativo.

El término 'homofobia' es utilizado de forma irónica y recurrente para describir cualquier tipo de inconveniente, frustración o bloqueo creativo, especialmente cuando afecta a artistas o a la creación de contenido LGTBQ+. Esta hipérbole se ha convertido en una expresión de humor dentro de la comunidad.

  • Tell me why i was halfway through drawing an artfight attack and my computer straight up crashed!! the homophobia.
  • I want to draw the little mouse in one of the letters, but i have to work, homophobia.
  • The fact that i cannot watch a show, cross stitch, draw and read all at the same time is homophobia actually unu.
  • Why the fuck are purple eyes not a possibility in real life. The homophobia.
  • I was drawing some chest injury alastor and my ipad ran out of battery the homophobia.

3. Reflexiones sobre la Homofobia Real en el Arte y la Comunidad.

Más allá del humor y los memes, el archivo también revela conversaciones serias sobre la homofobia real. Los artistas discuten cómo el prejuicio, tanto externo como internalizado, afecta su trabajo, sus vidas y la representación de la comunidad LGTBQ+ en el arte.

  • Colored pencil art piece meant to represent homophobia, transphobia, and overall violence and prejudice against any and all members of the lgbtq community.
  • Was drawing yaoi and said out loud "this is so wrong" ?? internalized homophobia or catholic guilt, call it.
  • Here’s something that many people may be surprised by but some of the worst homophobia i’ve ever experienced has been by nyc art people.
  • Interesting! and unfortunately unsurprising. Of course they would come for art to justify the explicit homophobia. Because artists and the queer community often have a very close relationship.
  • If an artist refuses to draw a same-sex couple in a romantic non-sexual situation, like a dinner date, would you consider that homophobia?
